EXTREME EPSILON COMPARISON: ε=32/255 vs ε=8/255
================================================================================
Date: 2026-03-13
Algorithm: FGSM only (PGD not yet run)
Dataset: RSNA 200 samples (155 paired for random_patch mode)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. MOTIVATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Previous experiments (ε=1,2,4,8,16/255) showed paradoxical pattern:
- Under L∞ constraint: Random/Full ASR > Lesion ASR
- Hypothesis: Larger attack surface compensates for lack of semantic targeting

This extreme epsilon test (ε=32/255 = 12.5% of pixel range) explores:
- Does the paradox persist at visually-obvious perturbation levels?
- What is the cost-benefit tradeoff at extreme epsilons?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. FGSM ε=32/255 RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MODE: LESION
  Sample size:  200
  ASR:          10.5% ← SURPRISINGLY LOW despite extreme ε!
  Avg L2:       15.570
  Avg L∞:       0.12549 (exactly 32/255, constraint satisfied)
  Avg L0:       ~19,789 pixels modified
  Avg Eff:      0.000579

MODE: RANDOM_PATCH
  Sample size:  155 (45 failures due to rigid translation constraints)
  ASR:          22.6% ← 2.15× higher than lesion!
  Avg L2:       48.628 (3.12× larger than lesion)
  Avg L∞:       0.12549
  Avg L0:       ~150,000 pixels
  Avg Eff:      0.000261

MODE: FULL
  Sample size:  200
  ASR:          19.0% ← 1.81× higher than lesion
  Avg L2:       48.628
  Avg L∞:       0.12549
  Avg L0:       ~150,000 pixels (all pixels)
  Avg Eff:      0.000264

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

✓ L∞ PARADOX PERSISTS EVEN AT EXTREME EPSILON:
  - Despite using 32/255 (visually obvious distortions), lesion attacks
    achieve LOWEST success rate (10.5%)
  - Random patch attacks achieve HIGHEST success rate (22.6%)
  - The advantage of larger attack surface dominates semantic specificity

✓ EFFICIENCY vs EFFECTIVENESS TRADEOFF:
  - Lesion attacks: HIGH efficiency (0.000579) but LOW ASR (10.5%)
  - Random attacks: LOW efficiency (0.000261) but HIGH ASR (22.6%)
  - Confirms: L∞ constraint favors "brute force" over "surgical strikes"

✓ ENERGY COST DISPARITY:
  - Lesion L2 = 15.57 (localized, small area)
  - Random/Full L2 = 48.63 (distributed, large area)
  - 3.12× energy difference, yet random achieves 2.15× higher ASR!

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. COMPARISON WITH ε=8/255 BASELINE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
(Note: ε=8/255 data from results/unified_final_rigid_translation/)

Expected pattern based on previous epsilon sweep (ε=1,2,4,8,16/255):
- As ε increases: ASR increases, efficiency decreases
- Lesion consistently underperforms random/full under L∞ constraint

Hypothesis for ε=8/255 vs ε=32/255:
- ε=8/255:  Lesion ~5-8% ASR,   Random ~15-20% ASR
- ε=32/255: Lesion 10.5% ASR,   Random 22.6% ASR
- Trend: Larger ε → higher ASR, but gap remains (random > lesion)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. SCIENTIFIC IMPLICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

❌ L∞ METRIC FAILS TO VALIDATE LESION VULNERABILITY HYPOTHESIS:
   - Even at extreme perturbations, lesions are NOT more vulnerable
   - Contradicts medical intuition that disease features are fragile
   - Suggests L∞ is wrong tool for measuring "semantic vulnerability"

✓ CONFIRMS METRIC-DEPENDENT CONCLUSIONS:
   - L∞ dimension: Random > Lesion (area advantage)
   - L2 dimension: Lesion > Random (from C&W/DeepFool results)
   - Underscores critical importance of metric selection in robustness

✓ PRACTICAL DEFENSE INSIGHTS:
   - L∞ robustness training may inadvertently protect lesion regions more
   - Adversaries constrained by L∞ should target full image, not lesions
   - Medical AI defenses should focus on L2/semantic metrics, not L∞
